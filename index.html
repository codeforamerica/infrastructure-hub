  1. Welcome to ATL Vision 2015!

    The City of Atlanta is planning a $250 million bond measure to invest in both transportation and facility infrastructure. Citizen input is a very important part of this process, and this website has been to give citizens a place to provide feedback throughout all phases of the project. The city will use this feedback to determine project selection criteria, narrow down the list of final projects, and to update citizens on progress.

  2. What is the 2015 infrastructure bond?

    Atlanta’s infrastructure is aging, which presents challenges for both public safety and maintenance. In fact, as of mid-2014, the city has identified an infrastructure backlog of over 1 billion dollars.

    This backlog consists of $881 million dollars worth of transportation projects, $41 million of fleet upgrades and replacements, and $131 million dollars worth of facility projects. Of this billion-dollar backlog, approximately $305 million (combined) is considered high priority.

    In an effort to reduce this backlog and invest in making Atlanta an even better place to live, work, bike, walk and drive, the city hopes to issue a $250 million dollar municipal bond dedicated to both transportation and facility infrastructure.

  3. How will the infrastructure bond be funded?

    This bond will NOT raise taxes for citizens. Instead, it will be funded through a combination of cost savings and revenue generation in the FY2015 budget. Mayor Kasim Reed recently appointed a Commission on Waste and Efficiency in Government to identify potential cost savings areas, and the city’s Innovation Delivery and Performance Team is also focused on areas where the city can generate new revenue streams.

    Depending on the cost savings and revenue identified, the infrastructure bond may exceed $250 million, meaning that the city will be able to implement even more important projects for the public. However, even if the bond amount increases, the bond will NOT increase taxes.
